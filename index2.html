Neutral Grounding (Earthing) Resistors

Neutral Grounding (Earthing) Resistors – called Neutral Grounding Resistors (NGRs) – are employed in AC distribution networks to limit the current that would flow through the neutral point of a transformer or generator in the event of an earth fault.

NERs limit fault currents to a value that does not cause any further damage to switchgear, generators or transformers beyond what has already been caused by the fault itself, and in some cases limit touch and step potentials to safer values than a solidly grounded systems. In addition, consideration is given to operation of protection relays with CT’s within the required time.

Enclosure Materials and Design

The standard enclosure material is Sheet steel Minimum 2 mm thickness with an epoxy powder coat finish. Alternative materials include both SS-304 & SS-316 stainless steel.

As standard we offer IP55 enclosures. They are suitable for outdoor use whilst also allowing good ventilation. We also supply IP33 and IP54 enclosures. When side mounted cable termination chambers are specified they are IP54 or IP55 as standard.

Whilst our standard range of enclosures are suitable for most applications, we are also happy to provide custom designed equipment if your application requires it.
